Mid-benchmark week. Gemini CLI just shut down. Not deprecated, not rate-limited, just gone.

I had a benchmark suite half-run and an evaluation engine that didn't exist anymore.

No panic post, no "pivoting to opportunity" spin. It was just annoying. I lost a day figuring out what could replace it without adding another cloud dependency I'd have to babysit.

Landed on MiMo. Xiaomi's open-source CLI-native model, MIT license, OpenAI-compatible API. Ran it through two validation passes before trusting it with real benchmark data.

The lesson isn't "always have a backup." It's that zero-cost, local-first infrastructure means you're accepting that pieces of your stack can vanish overnight, and you need to be able to swap them without the whole system falling over. That's the actual tradeoff nobody puts in the pitch.
