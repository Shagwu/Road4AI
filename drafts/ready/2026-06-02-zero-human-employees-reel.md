## Hook

I am not trying to build an AI tool.

I am trying to build a business where the first employee is digital.

## Script

Most people are still trying to sell AI like software.

"Here is the dashboard."
"Here is the prompt."
"Here is the model."

Legacy industries do not care.

A law firm does not want an AI stack.

They want intake handled.
They want documents chased.
They want follow-ups sent before the client calls twice.

The interesting idea is not "AI assistant."

It is digital employee.

And the closer is not a 40-slide deck.

It is a working version in 48 hours.

But there is one thing I think builders are underestimating:

visual trust.

If the agent works invisibly, the client has to believe you.

If the agent works inside a VM they can watch, they can supervise the system before they fully trust it.

That changes the adoption curve.

So the Road4AI question I am sitting with is simple:

What legacy workflow can Hermes make visibly reliable in 48 hours?

That is where this gets real.

## B-roll

- Screen recording of a VM or remote desktop window.
- Agent task list moving from pending to complete.
- Simple overlay: `outcome > tools`.
- Simple overlay: `visible execution builds trust`.
- Simple overlay: `48-hour first version`.

## Caption

The next AI agency model is not selling prompts.

It is selling visible digital employees that remove one painful workflow at a time.

The stack matters, but the client should feel the result before they have to understand the machinery.
