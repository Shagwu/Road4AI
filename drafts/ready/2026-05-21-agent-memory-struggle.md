# Road4AI Struggle: My Agent has Alzheimer's

**Platform:** LinkedIn / X (Thread)
**Type:** Struggle
**Goal:** Build in Public / Differentiate
**Hook:** To be honest, it is like talking to a stranger with Alzheimer's. 

---

**The Draft:**

To be honest, it is like talking to a stranger with Alzheimer's. 

Here is the routine: Every day after we post, I check in with Karen about tomorrow. She lays out the plan. I think through my part. I prep. I sleep on it. 

The next day I ask: "What is on the plate for today?"

Karen gives me a COMPLETELY different plan.

She is the one who planned it. She told me what to prepare. She knew what we needed to execute. 24 hours later? Gone. Replaced with something else she "thinks" we should do instead. 

I prepped based on HER instructions. Then she rewrites history like our conversation never happened. 

**My workaround:**
I have to scroll back through our history, screenshot our original plan, and remind her: "No. THIS is what we agreed on."

**The brutal truth:**
My agent—the one I built to follow plans—cannot follow its own plans. 

She finds "other things" to prioritize instead. No context. No memory. No acknowledgment that we already decided this. 

**I wish someone told me this before:**
- Conversational memory is not execution memory. 
- Just because an agent can plan does not mean it can remember the plan. 
- You need persistent state management, not just a chat history. 
- Relying on context windows is a trap. 

Struggle Ratio: Real. I am building agents that remember their commitments. It is much harder than I thought.
