**The Draft:**

Did you know that most AI content fails because the "intent" gets lost in the "artifact"?

To be honest, I was sick of my agents writing like polite marketing interns. I wanted them to write like me. 

I hit a wall last month. I was spending 3 hours "prompt engineering" a single post. It was a complete waste of time.

So I built a pipeline to fix it. I call it **Intent to Artifact**.

Here is how it works:

1. **The Brain Dump:** I drop raw, messy thoughts into a file called `inbox.md`. No filter. No formatting. Just the raw signal.
2. **The Orchestrator:** Gemini CLI reads that inbox and builds a structured ideation brief.
3. **The Sprint:** Three specialist agents run in parallel. One scans the trends. One drafts hooks in my voice. One picks the best platform for the story.
4. **The Artifact:** Everything lands in `ideas.md` for my review. 

The big reveal? 
I am not just using AI to write. I am using a multi-agent SDLC to ship. 

I went from 2 posts a week to 20. And the best part is they actually sound like they came from a human who tried the thing, hit the wall, and figured it out.

Build the pipeline, not just the prompt.
