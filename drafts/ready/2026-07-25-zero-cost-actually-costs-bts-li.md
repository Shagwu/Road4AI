"Zero-cost" sounds like a deal until you're the one paying in a different currency: your own time babysitting local models.

Running qwen2.5-coder and mistral-nemo locally through Ollama means no API bill. It also means you're the one managing memory, watching for drift, rerunning evals when a model's outputs shift, and debugging why something worked yesterday and doesn't today.

Cloud APIs sell you convenience. Local-first sells you control. Neither one is free, they just charge you in different ways.

I'd still pick local-first for this project. But I stopped pretending it's the easy path. It's the path where you own every layer, which means you also own every failure.
