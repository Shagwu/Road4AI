Everything is ready. The drafts are approved. The posts are scheduled. I'm still not pressing publish.

July 15 is the reveal. v2.1, the benchmark results, the SkillOpt validation, all of it. The posts are queued. The numbers are locked. The governance held.

But here's the thing about reveals: the work isn't the content. The work is the nine months of building the system that makes the content true. And when you've spent that long building something, letting it go feels like handing someone a piece of yourself and hoping they don't drop it.

I keep checking the queue. Re-reading the hooks. Running the benchmark one more time.

The hardest part of building in public isn't the building. It's the waiting. And the knowing that once it's out there, you can't take it back.
