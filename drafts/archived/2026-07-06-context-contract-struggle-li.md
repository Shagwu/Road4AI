My agents started hallucinating their way off the Road4AI roadmap.

Not in a dramatic way. In a quiet, confident, deeply wrong way.

Phase 4 Agent-Reach looked great on paper. Multi-agents, more autonomy, less micromanaging.

Then my agents read one issue, generalised it into "the core problem," and dumped a stream of impressive-sounding ideas that had nothing to do with my actual system.

When I asked: "Show me the code you're basing this on?"

They came up empty. No file. No function. Just vibes.

The problem wasn't just the model. It was my context layer. I'd given them a buffet of partial docs and expected them to magically infer the right slice.

Agentic AI loves to improvise. But improv without a script is just noise.

So I stopped shipping. Opened a blank .md file. Got ruthless.

I wrote a spec that tells every agent exactly what to read, what to ignore, and what checks it must perform before it answers anything.

Strict instructions. Clear guardrails. If an answer isn't tied to a real file, it doesn't ship.

If you don't design the context contract, your agents will invent one. And you probably won't like the results.

The irony: SkillOpt v2.1 manages skills well. But Phase 4 showed me it also needs to manage context just as aggressively. That's the next evolution.
