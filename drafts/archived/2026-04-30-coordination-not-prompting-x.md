# The Real Job of an AI Engineer Is Coordination

**Target Platform**: Twitter/X
**Status**: Ready for review
**Scheduled Date**: 2026-04-30

## Hook
Prompting is the shallow end.
Coordination is the job.

## Body
### Post 1
I think the phrase "prompt engineer" trained people to optimize the wrong layer.

The hard part of agent systems is rarely the prompt.

It is coordination.

### Post 2
Can the agents share state?
Can they recover from failure?
Can they validate their work?
Can they hand off cleanly between planning and execution?

That is where the real engineering lives.

### Post 3
In Road4AI, the useful pieces are not mysterious:

- state files
- memory
- routing
- tool boundaries
- validation loops

That is what makes the workflow durable.

### Post 4
A strong prompt can improve a weak system a little.

A strong coordination model can improve the whole stack:

- fewer repeated mistakes
- clearer responsibilities
- easier debugging
- less operator guesswork

### Post 5
So when I think about AI engineering now, I think less about clever phrasing and more about protocol design.

Good agents are not just well-prompted.

They are well-coordinated.
