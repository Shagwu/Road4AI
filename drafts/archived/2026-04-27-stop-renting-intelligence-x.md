# Stop Renting Intelligence

**Target Platform**: Twitter/X
**Status**: Ready for review
**Scheduled Date**: 2026-04-27

## Hook
Most AI products are not infrastructure.
They are rent.

## Body
### Post 1
Most AI products are not infrastructure.

They are monthly rent on a web UI wrapper.

If your workflow disappears when you cancel the subscription, you do not own the system.

### Post 2
That is the hidden cost of the current AI stack:

- recurring spend
- no visibility into how it works
- no reusable operator muscle

Convenience is fine.
Dependence is not.

### Post 3
Road4AI exists because I wanted the opposite:

- local-first workflows
- CLI-native control
- free tiers where they are good enough
- explicit architecture instead of dashboard theater

### Post 4
The question I care about is not:
"Which AI tool should I subscribe to?"

It is:
"Which parts of this stack can I actually own?"

That shift changes how you build.

### Post 5
If your AI workflow needs a vendor's interface to exist, you are renting intelligence.

If it runs from your repo, your terminal, your memory layer, and your orchestration logic, you are building an asset.

That is the line.
