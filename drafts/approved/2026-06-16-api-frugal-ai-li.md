# Draft: The API-Frugal AI
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Type:** Win / Build in Public
**Hook:** I stopped chasing the smartest models and started building a system that can outlast them.

---

To be honest, I was caught in a trap.

I was spending 20% of my free API quota just to check the status of my own agents. Every time I hit "run," I was watching my credits vanish.

It made me realize something about the way we are taught to build with AI. We are told to always use the biggest, smartest, and most expensive model for everything.

That is how you build a demo. It is not how you build a business that lasts.

I decided to pivot to what I call the API-Frugal AI. 

I stopped trying to brute force my way through with money. Instead, I started using local-first, zero-cost workflows.

Here is what my stack looks like now:
- Gemini CLI for the heavy research and ideation.
- Ollama running local models for the "grunt work" like JSON formatting and structure.
- OpenCode Work for a local coding assistant that does not cost a cent to run.

The result? I hit 181ms on my first query today. It was fast, it was local, and it was free.

I realized that expensive models are just specialists. If you want to scale, you do not need more specialists. You need a resilient system and the skill to drive it.

Constraint is not a bug. It is a feature that forces you to build better architecture.

Are you building for the next credit cycle, or are you building for the long haul?
