# LinkedIn Draft: Token Anxiety

I caught myself rationing ideas.

Not money.
Not time.

Ideas.

That is the weirdest side effect of limited AI request windows.

You do not just think:

"I have fewer messages left."

You start thinking:

"Is this thought worth spending one?"

That is a dangerous question for a builder.

Because the best ideas usually do not arrive clean.

They show up messy.
Half-formed.
Annoying.
Too early.
Right in the middle of something else.

And the correct move is to capture them before they disappear.

But on June 2, I felt the hesitation.

A fresh idea was clear enough to plan with an LLM.

Then the system slowed down, threw the "taking longer than usual" moment, and broke the flow.

The idea did not vanish all at once.

It got harder to hold.

That is the part people miss when they talk about AI limits.

The cost is not just waiting.

The cost is behavioral.

You become conservative at the exact moment you should be exploratory.

You start saving the next request instead of capturing the messy thought.

You turn the tool into a scarce resource, and suddenly your brain starts optimizing around the scarcity.

That is token anxiety.

Not fear of tokens.

Fear of losing momentum because the tool makes you negotiate with the moment.

I do not think the answer is "unlimited everything."

The answer is better capture discipline.

If the idea is alive, write it down outside the tool first.

Then use the model when the thought has survived long enough to be worth shaping.

The builder's job is not to protect the request window.

The builder's job is to protect the signal.
