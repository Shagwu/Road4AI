## Problem section

### Heading

The SaaS tax is eating the AI stack.

### Body

Most AI products sell convenience by renting your workflow back to you. You pay monthly for a web UI wrapper, lose control of the environment, and end up with a stack you cannot fully inspect or own.

Road4AI takes the opposite position. Use local tools. Use free tiers where they make sense. Keep the workflow close to the terminal. Treat AI like infrastructure, not entertainment.

## Value proposition

### Heading

Built for operators, not passengers.

### Body

Road4AI gives you a practical blueprint for running AI workflows with lower cost, more control, and clearer system boundaries.

### Benefit blocks

#### Zero-cost by default

Prefer local models and free tiers before paid subscriptions become the default answer.

#### Own the workflow

Run a stack you can inspect, script, version, and evolve on your own terms.

#### Verify before you trust

Audit downloaded or forked repositories with a Magika-based verifier that flags mismatches and suspicious binaries.

#### Learn a repeatable system

Road4AI is not just a repo. It is a model for how technical builders can operate AI workflows without vendor dependence.

## How it works

### Heading

From repo to operator stack in three steps.

### Steps

#### 1. Inspect the philosophy

Read the manifesto and system blueprint to understand the zero-cost-first model.

#### 2. Verify the codebase

Use `verify_repo.py` to scan a repository and catch file mismatches or unexpected binaries before execution.

#### 3. Build on owned infrastructure

Run local-first tools, free-tier models, and MCP-connected services inside a workflow you control.
